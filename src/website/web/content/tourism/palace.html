About King Fuad I Palace

Kafr El Sheikh Governorate is one of the governorates with a rich heritage of both ancient Egyptian and Islamic antiquities, including King Fouad's Palace and its annexes in Kafr El Sheikh.

King Fouad held Kafr El Sheikh in a special place, as it contains numerous historical monuments dating back to the royal era in general, and the era of King Fouad in particular. Kafr El Sheikh was named the Fouadiya Directorate after him.

One of the most important of these monuments is the Palace of King Fouad I, located east of Kafr El Sheikh, just steps from Kafr El Sheikh General Hospital. This palace was where the king resided during his visits to Kafr El Sheikh with his family. Built in the European (Italian-French) style, it was constructed in 1934 AD.
