About Edfina Bridges

It helped save over a billion cubic meters of water that would otherwise have been discharged into the sea. Its construction was intended to prevent water from seeping into the sea when the water level dropped following each flood. The Edfina Barrage is the only barrage that blocks the waters of the Rosetta Branch of the Nile from the Mediterranean Sea.

Edfina Barrage is located at the end of Edfina village, Rashid district, Beheira Governorate, and is considered the main passageway linking the governorates of Kafr El Sheikh, Beheira, and Alexandria, via Matoubas district, Kafr El Sheikh Governorate, and Rashid district, Beheira Governorate.

There are five gardens spread across the Edfina Barrage on the left bank, with an area of ​​25 acres, and on the right bank there are two gardens built on ten acres, which contain a nursery for producing rare flowers and plants, green spaces, basins, geometric shapes, fountains, and two gardens with an area of ​​100 acres planted with citrus fruits and fruits. The Barrage is located 20 km south of the city of Rashid and represents a tourist site and attraction.
