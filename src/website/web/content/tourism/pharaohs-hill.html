About Hill of the Pharaohs

Tell el-Fara'in is a group of three archaeological mounds located northeast of the city of Desouk in northern Egypt, specifically next to the village of Ibtu and approximately 3 kilometers from the village of El-Ajouzin. The area includes the archaeological site of the ancient city of Buto.

Excavations are usually ongoing, with Egyptian and foreign missions permanently residing there. One of the most important discoveries resulting from excavations in January 2018 was an archaeological discovery consisting of remains of mud brick walls attributed to the ancient Egyptian civilization and statues believed to belong to King Psamtik I.

Tell el-Fara'in is the site of the ancient city of Buto, which was the capital of the northern kingdom before the unification of Pharaonic Egypt. King Narmer launched a military attack on it to annex it to his southern kingdom and unify Egypt. This happened in 3200 BC.
