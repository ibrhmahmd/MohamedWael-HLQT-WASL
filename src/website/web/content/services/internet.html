Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Why is my internet so slow?

There could be several reasons for slow internet speed, including network congestion, issues with your modem or router, the type of internet plan you have, or problems with your internet service provider's infrastructure.

My internet is not working at all. What should I do?

First, try restarting your modem and router. Unplug them from the power outlet, wait about 30 seconds, and then plug them back in. If the problem persists, contact your internet service provider's technical support.

What is Wi-Fi and how do I connect?

Wi-Fi is a wireless networking technology that allows devices to connect to the internet without cables. To connect, find the Wi-Fi network name (SSID) in your device's Wi-Fi settings and enter the password (if required).

I can't connect to specific websites. Is the internet down?

It's possible the website itself is experiencing issues. Try accessing other websites to check if your general internet connection is working. If only specific sites are unreachable, the problem likely lies with those sites.

What does "bandwidth" mean?

Bandwidth refers to the maximum amount of data that can be transferred over an internet connection in a given amount of time. Higher bandwidth generally means faster internet speeds and the ability to handle more data-intensive activities.

How do I secure my Wi-Fi network?

To secure your Wi-Fi, use a strong, unique password (WPA3 is recommended if your router supports it), enable network encryption, and consider hiding your network name (SSID). You can usually configure these settings through your router's administration interface.

What is a modem and what is a router?

A modem connects your home network to the internet service provider's network. A router distributes the internet connection from the modem to multiple devices within your home, often wirelessly via Wi-Fi. Some devices combine both modem and router functionalities.

My online video keeps buffering. How can I fix this?

Buffering can occur due to a slow internet connection, network congestion, or the video streaming service's server load. Try lowering the video quality, pausing the video for a few moments to allow it to load, or ensuring no other devices are heavily using your internet connection.

What is a VPN and should I use one?

A VPN (Virtual Private Network) extends a private network across a public network, allowing users to send and receive data across shared or public networks as if their computing devices were directly connected to the private network. It can enhance privacy and security, especially on public Wi-Fi. Whether you should use one depends on your security and privacy needs.

How do I find my Wi-Fi password?

You can usually find your Wi-Fi password on a sticker on your router. If you've changed it, you can typically find it in your router's administration interface, which you access through a web browser by entering your router's IP address.
